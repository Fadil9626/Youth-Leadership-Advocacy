Online Introduction of Youth Leadership and Advocacy (YLA)

Solomon Christian Kekurah is a Barrister and Solicitor of the High Court of Sierra Leone and a member of the Sierra Leone Bar Association from Bonthe Island, Southern Area of the Republic of Sierra Leone. He started his early academic journey in his hometown of Bonthe, at the Bonthe Amalgamated Primary School where he sat to his N.P.S.E Exams and later left for Freetown in the early 2000s to pursue his Junior Secondary School Education. Solomon did his Junior Secondary Education at the Baptist Secondary School, Mayemie, Allen Town, Freetown, and sat to the B.E.C.E in 2006. He has enrolled at Christ the King College in 2006 for his Senior Secondary School Education. In 2009, Solomon sat to the West African Senior Secondary School Certificate Examination (W.A.S.S.C.E) and gained admission into the University of Makeni (UNIMAK) Law Department for the academic year of 2009/2010 to do his undergraduate degree in Law (LLB Hons) where he acquired a diploma in Paralegal Studies and a Bachelor’s Degree in Law (LLB Hons). On the 13th of February 2013, Solomon was awarded a Diploma in Paralegal Studies with Division Two (II) Upper from the University of Makeni. On the 13th February 2016, he was awarded a Bachelor of Laws with Honours (LL B Hons) Division II (Upper Second Class) where he was one of three (3) best graduating law students of the year. He also grabbed the prize for best Law Dissertation of the year. Solomon applied to the Sierra Leone Law School in his final year of his undergraduate and was admitted into the school on October 17, 2016, to pursue his professional studies and to sit to the Bar Final Examination. Solomon was awarded and conferred with an Utter Barrister at Law (BL) and called to the Sierra Leone Bar by the Council of Legal Education, Sierra Leone Law School on the 20th October 2017. Solomon did his pupillage at the Jenkins-Johnston & Co Law Firm where he practiced as a Private Legal Practitioner until November 2020. Solomon applied and was accepted to work as a State Counsel in the Law Officers’ Department, Office of the Attorney-General & Ministry of Justice on the 1st of November, 2020 and continues to serve in that capacity. In 2019, Solomon was recognized and awarded with a certificate in the House of Parliament as a Youth Ambassador and one of 500 Most Influential Young Generational African Leaders in Sierra Leone by the Deputy Hon. Minister of Youth. He has attended several conferences, seminars, and workshops with a plethora of certificates in his cabinet.
