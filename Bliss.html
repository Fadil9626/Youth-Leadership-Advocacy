Online Introduction of Youth Leadership and Advocacy (YLA)

Meet the Secretary-General of Youth Leadership & Advocacy, Mr. Ibrahim Khalil Koroma aka Bliss. Ibrahim commonly known as Bliss grew up in Kenema and attended the Government Secondary School Kenema where he served as the Assistant Head Boy Bliss is a passionate change agent, advocate, youth activist, and pan Africanist. He has participated in and won series of debates in his childhood days, winner of the Best Child Broadcaster 2016 for the Children Forum Network in Kenema. At the University of Makeni, Mr. Ibrahim is a renowned Mooter, Debater, and a Public Speaker with vast experience in the International System of Debating. Recently, he participated in the My Intellectual Property Law Guide Online Debate Competition (A Continental Law Debate) and attained the Second Runner Up. Despite serving as the current Secretary-General of Youth Leadership & Advocacy, he is the erstwhile Deputy Attorney General of the Student’s Representative Council, UNIMAK, former De-Facto Coordinator of the Pen Foundation, and a member of the Campus Peace Club. Mr. Ibrahim has a strong value for his principles, and passion for Pan Africanism, he's a dedicated and proud Muslim and a strategic founding member of the Muslim Youth Assembly Sierra Leone, and founding Member of Rebirth of Pan Africanism (ROPA). Mr. Ibrahim is a Final Year Law Student at the University of Makeni who doubles as the Class Prefect. [11:01, 5/2/2021] Harash: Meet the Deputy Executive Director for Youth Leadership & Advocacy Ms. Mariama Yansaneh. Ms. Yansaneh was born and raised in Sierra Leone. She attended Forum for Africa Women Educationalist Primary School ( F. A. W. E). Did her Junior and Secondary School at the Government Model Secondary School where she was served as the Senior Prefect during her Junior School and also served as the Head Girl at the Senior Level. At Model Secondary School, she was the President of the Government Secondary School News Journal, where she aimed at ensuring she start practicing as young journalists. She was able to work with different schools across the country to promote students journalism. Ms. Yansaneh worked at the Premier Media Radio on a Radio Program called "Pikin Voice” and Culture Radio to advocate for children that are less privileged. Her aim was to talk on behalf of children who are suffering and faced with sexual gender based violence. Moreover, Ms. Yansaneh gained admission at Fourah Bay College, University of Sierra Leone. Presently she's a third-year student pursuing a Bachelor’s degree in Social Work. At the University, She is a volunteer at Social Workers Sierra Leone, where they cater to support the less privileged people, people with disability, and those with mental issues, etc
